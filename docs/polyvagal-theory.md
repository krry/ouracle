Vagal state gives rise to possible emotional states which entail certain possible explanatory thoughts and interpretations of sensations. These thoughts can be assembled, like the flashes of dreams, into certain more sensible or coherent stories. When asked why we intend one way or another, the mind may offer the story most likely to be accepted. Polyvagal theory would reject the story, or the origin of intent, being primary in the process of behavior.

# At any moment in a vertebrate body, the vagus nerve sits in one of three states…

## Dorsal vagal state

- Energy toward the back
- Balled up like a pill bug
- Still, cold, immobile, afraid
- Low oxytocin, low dopamine
- nervous energy is concentrated toward the back of the body
- often correlates with a fear state, disconnection
- the body cools, freezes, immobilizes
- the mind slows and becomes stuck
- e.g., a coiled up opossum, playing dead

## Sympathetic vagal state

- Energy centered
- 4F response - fight, flight, fawn, freeze
- In motion, agitated, angry, frightened
- Dopamine seeking, serotonin low, high glucocorticoids
- nervous energy is held in the center of the body
- correlates with a 4F response - fawn, fight, flee, freeze (maybe not freeze)
- the body readies for action and tightens
- the mind becomes (overly) alert
- e.g., road rage or other highway driving states

## Ventral vagal state

- Energy forward
- Shoulders back, arms open, heart forward, belly exposed
- Calm, inviting connection, comfortable
- Oxytocin, dopamine, serotonin high
- nervous energy is presented toward the front of the body
- correlates with a state of openness, joy, connection
- the body warms, mobilizes, relaxes, wiggles
- the mind becomes pliable and playful
- e.g., the life of the party
